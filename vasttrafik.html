Prototyping for Västtrafik

Västtrafik To-Go allows you to search for bus trips within Västra Götaland county
and buy tickets for your travels.

Team.

Magnus Henry, Heliz Isa, Kristoffer Ödman.

Tools.

Figma.

Timeline.

3 weeks, winter 2023.

Brief.

In teams of four students, we were given the assignment
to find user friendly solutions for problems found
in Västtrafiks app “To-Go” and to make a prototype for those solutions.

Problems.

Through usability testing in a previous assignment, we discovered that the users had problems with;
1. Identifying the map covering the itinerary and the vehicle’s position.
2. Users had a hard time finding information about tickets for students.
3. The overview of the itinerary was messy and confusing.

Goals/Solutions.

Our solution for the problem with the confusion of the maps was to change the icon
which led to Google maps so that it more resembled the original icon of the red pin that is used by Google.
The reason for this was to create an association that would lead the user to understand were he/she would end up if pressing the icon.
We also made the icon and text that led to Västtrafiks own map easier to understand
by changing the button from “Show route” to “Show route and the vehicle’s position” We solved the problem with the lack of information about tickets for students
by adding another button to the selection so that there was not only a button for the adult ticket and children´s ticket
but also a button for students. We also changed the button that led to the information page from “traveling with kids” to “ticket information”. When it came to the overview of the itinerary, we tried to make it simple by creating a dropdown menu
showed each step of the trip separately.
But during the usability tests the users didn´t like that idea.
So, we decided to implement spacing and hierarchy to our layout to make it less messy but still keep the original design.
That left us a clean looking itinerary overview that the users fuound easier to interacted with.

Takeaways.

The most important things that I will take with me from this task are that your ideas might not always be as good as you want them to be
and that it is important not to put too much time into your wireframes that you are going to perform usability tests on.
Doing so might waste your time if the users don't like the idea, and you have to come up with another solution.
It is also important to create solutions based on facts
and not on what you believe is a good solution to the problem you are facing.
